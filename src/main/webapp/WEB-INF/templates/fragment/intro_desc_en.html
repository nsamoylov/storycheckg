This site I have built for myself, to test quality of my English translation of my first book written in Russian. I have translated first ten pages, and my American friend edited them. Then I quickly built this site (I worked as a software programmer for many years) and published both versions of translation - mine not edited and the edited one. Then I have sent an email to two groups of people: one group had to read not edited translation and another group - the one edited by my friend. After reading, they answered a few questions. These answers provided a great insight into the readers perception, bringing along quite a few surprises. That experience convinced me that the best way to gauge the market is to test it.

Sounds obvious? Yet, how often we do it?

I used this site to test other things - writing ideas, titles, even single phrases - with a great success too. After several my books were published, this method lost its importance of the only source of the market intelligence for me. I still use it, but not as often. Should I continue to invest time and money into the site maintenance? That would be fine, I thought, if other writers could use it for their benefit too. So, I added support for multiple accounts, to allow every registered user to access the same functionality I used to enjoy myself only.

Recently I have also added support for finding an editor and finding a translator, which I missed in days of my search for these services.

Look through the sample pages and see if this site could be of help for you.
